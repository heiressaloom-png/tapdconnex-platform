TAPDconnex Behavioral Intelligence v1
> How the analyzer reads behaviour as **evidence of movement**, not tone. This
> is the anti-inflation core. The deterministic analyzer (Increment B) enforces
> these rules; the prompt asks the LLM to extract the evidence they need.
1. Progression model (do not collapse into one "interest" score)
```
Connection  → creates possibility
Curiosity   → creates information exchange
Interest    → creates relevance
Intention   → creates possible direction
Commitment  → creates investment
Momentum    → creates actual progress
Outcome     → confirms result
```
Each is scored separately. A warm, curious conversation can be high on
connection/curiosity and still zero on momentum.
2. Non-negotiable principles
Words are signals; behaviour is evidence. "maybe / interesting / send me
something / let's connect / keep in touch / next quarter" are never read in
isolation — evaluate against ownership, specificity, timeline, access,
reciprocity, investment, follow-through.
Interest ≠ commitment. Commitment begins when the person invests
something (time, effort, information, access, reputation, resources,
ownership).
Tone ≠ intent. Tone supports a relationship read; it must not drive
opportunity scoring. Tone lives under Connection / Relational Texture.
Friendliness ≠ opportunity. Friendliness raises warmth only.
Momentum = state change. Something is different after than before
(meeting scheduled, intro offered, decision-maker named, owner assigned,
timeline clarified, access improved, blocker surfaced, follow-up completed).
Specificity beats vocabulary. No naive rules ("maybe = bad", "send deck =
strong"). Ask: who owns it? what exactly happens next? when? why? who else?
what after that? did they follow through?
Pattern over motive. Never "they were fake/lying/manipulative". Say "the
interaction stayed vague / commitment is weak / no ownership was created /
words were positive but behaviour hasn't supported them yet."
3. Commitment hierarchy (cost = evidence)
Low-cost (≈ warmth, not commitment): compliments, friendliness, card
exchange, LinkedIn connect, "send me something", "interesting", "let's connect".
Medium-cost: specific review request, named meeting, clear next step,
direct contact for a purpose, time commitment, explicit follow-up by a date.
High-cost: intro to decision-maker, internal stakeholder access, sharing
internal context, taking responsibility, putting reputation behind the user,
forwarding internally.
Highest-cost: budget discussion, paid pilot, formal proposal request,
contract / purchase / sponsorship / hiring process.
Rule: compliments cost nothing; generic openness costs little; commitment
requires investment.
4. Deflection patterns (deterministic)
Polite Buffer — "interesting / nice to meet / we should connect" → warmth
possible, commitment low.
Material Sink — "send me something / send the deck / send the link" →
weak unless purpose + owner + timeline + destination + next action.
Elastic Future — "maybe next quarter / later this year / when things
settle" → deferral unless a clear re-entry path.
Protective Blocker — "blocked until October, come back after budget
review, Sarah owns the vendor list" → not active now, but meaningful
(blocker + timing + owner exist) → Deferred with Pathway.
Delegated Fog — "someone in the team may want this / speak to someone" →
weak unless a named person or route exists.
Perpetual Evaluation — "we're considering / exploring / looking into it"
→ exploration unless process + stage + timeline are clear.
Broken Micro-Promise — "I'll send the invite tomorrow" then no invite →
contradiction / downgrade.
Repeated Delay — "busy / later / not now" repeated without an alternative
→ avoidance / low priority.
5. Behavioral read labels (the headline verdict)
```
Active Pursuit          — clear owner + next step + timing/access + commitment/investment
Mutual Exploration      — two-way questioning + relevance + some specificity, not yet committed
Polite Openness         — warm/friendly/positive, low investment
Future Deferral         — future timing dominates, no strong immediate action
Deferred with Pathway   — not active now, but blocker + timing + owner exist
Low Engagement          — little specificity/reciprocity, no movement
Stalled / Contradicted  — positive words not followed by behaviour
```
6. Specificity & contradiction rules
Specificity raises commitment/momentum: named people, exact dates,
defined processes, explicit owners, concrete destinations.
Contradiction lowers them: a stated promise with no corresponding
behaviour/outcome → momentum downgrade, status may become Stalled.
7. Confidence model
```
Observed system event / explicit transcript evidence /
structured commitment w/ owner+date     → high
Contextual inference                    → medium
Positive language only / tone only /
vibe only                               → low (never high)
```
8. Worked examples (acceptance anchors)
"Interesting, send me something. Maybe next quarter." → connection medium,
commitment low, momentum low, read = Polite Openness / Future Deferral.
"Send it by Friday. I'll forward it to Sarah in HR; we're reviewing vendors
next week." → warmth medium/low, commitment high, momentum high,
access + stakeholder present, read = Active Pursuit / Mutual Exploration.
"Lovely meeting you, so interesting, let's keep in touch." → connection
high, commitment low, momentum low, light follow-up, no opportunity overstatement.
"Blocked this quarter; come back after October budget review. Sarah owns the
vendor list." → Future Deferral with Pathway, follow-up later, not dead.
"I'll send the invite tomorrow" → (no invite) → contradiction, momentum
downgraded, status Stalled, advise not to over-invest.
"Lovely chat about travel and family; warm and open, but nothing tied to my
focus." → connection high, linkage low, opportunity low; read = good
human connection, weak strategic alignment.
9. Anti-inflation rules (summary)
Warmth weight ≈ 5%. Follow-through/specificity/investment/ownership/access
dominate.
Interest cannot become momentum without a state change.
Opportunity readiness is capped by commitment.
Human Reads describe these verdicts; they never raise them.
Language to the user is human, never raw numbers: not "Commitment: 22" but
"this stayed warm but low-commitment — no clear owner, timing, or next action
yet."
